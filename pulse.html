How to Read the Pulse — Plain English Guide
What each panel means and exactly what to do with it
⚡
OI SPURTERS — What does it mean?
Open Interest suddenly building up

What happened: Institutions just bought or sold a large number of new contracts at this stock. OI is the total number of open positions — when it spikes, new money is entering.

Who did this: Mutual funds, FIIs, HNIs — the big players. They don't trade small. A 20%+ OI spike in 1 session = significant institutional activity.

✅ Bullish OI spike: Price rising + OI rising = fresh longs. Institutions are BUYING. Trend likely continues.

⚠️ Bearish OI spike: Price falling + OI rising = fresh shorts. Institutions are SELLING. Downtrend likely continues.

💡 What to do:
Check if price is also moving in the same direction as OI. If yes — consider following the institutional direction. If price is flat but OI is building — a big move is coming, direction TBD.
📉
OI REDUCERS — What does it mean?
Open Interest suddenly dropping

What happened: Existing positions are being closed out. Traders who were long are selling, or traders who were short are buying back. Either way — someone is EXITING.

Two very different scenarios:

Short Covering: Price rising + OI falling = shorts buying back at a loss. This creates a quick sharp spike up but is NOT a real trend — it exhausts itself fast.

Long Unwinding: Price falling + OI falling = longs cutting losses and exiting. This can signal the end of an uptrend — the bulls are giving up.

💡 What to do:
If you're holding a position in this stock — be cautious. The smart money is exiting. Don't add fresh positions based on OI reducers alone. Wait for clarity.
📈
PRICE + OI BUILD — What does it mean?
The strongest possible signal

What happened: Price went up AND new contracts were added simultaneously. This is the gold standard confirmation — the move has real institutional money behind it.

The 4 OI combinations — memorise these:

Price↑ + OI↑
Long buildup — strong bull. Follow it.
Price↓ + OI↑
Short buildup — strong bear. Follow it.
Price↑ + OI↓
Short covering — weak move. Don't chase.
Price↓ + OI↓
Long unwinding — trend ending. Exit longs.
💡 What to do:
Price↑ + OI↑ stocks from this panel are your highest quality setups of the day. Open their full chain in the Stocks page to find the entry level and OI walls.

F&O BAN LIST — Critical to know
Why some stocks get banned and what it means for you

What is an F&O ban? NSE bans a stock from new F&O positions when its total OI crosses 95% of the Market-Wide Position Limit (MWPL). This is a safety mechanism to prevent excessive speculation.

⚠️ During a ban: You CANNOT open new positions. You can only square off (close) existing ones. If you open a new position in a banned stock, NSE charges a heavy penalty.

When does the ban lift? When total OI drops below 80% of MWPL — usually takes 1-3 days as people exit.

💡 What to do:
Check this page every morning before trading. Any stock showing 🚫 BAN — skip it completely for new trades that day. The Stocks page also flags banned stocks in red.

Advanced Strategies — Natenberg Framework
When to use each strategy based on IV + OI + DTE
🟢 Low IV Environment (<14%)
Options are cheap like discounted movie tickets. Buy before the show starts.

Best strategies:
• Buy ATM CE or PE (directional)
• Long Straddle before events
• Debit Spreads (buy higher, sell lower strike)
• Calendar Spread (buy far expiry, sell near)

Logic: When IV is low, vega is your friend. If IV expands, your option gains value even if price doesn't move.
🟡 Normal IV (14-20%)
Fair value environment. Direction matters more than volatility.

Best strategies:
• Bull/Bear Call or Put Spreads
• Iron Condor (stable markets)
• Covered Call (if holding stock)
• Synthetic Long/Short (high conviction)

Logic: Defined-risk spreads work best here — capped loss, decent reward, theta working for you on spreads.
🔴 High IV (>20%)
Options are expensive like peak season flights. Sell before prices fall.

Best strategies:
• Straddle/Strangle Sell (collect premium)
• Iron Condor (wider wings)
• Credit Spreads (sell the expensive strike)
• Cash-secured Put (if want to buy stock cheap)

Logic: When IV is high, vega works against buyers. Sellers collect elevated premiums and wait for IV to fall.
⚡ IV Crush — The Most Common Beginner Mistake
You buy a Nifty CE before RBI policy. RBI announces rate cut — Nifty goes up 200pts. But your CE option LOSES money. How?

Before the event: IV was 28% (high fear, expensive options). After the event: IV crashes to 14% (mystery solved, no more fear). Your option's vega exposure makes it lose ₹500 per lot from IV crush — more than it gained from the 200pt move.

Natenberg's lesson: Before events, buy options early when IV is still low. Or sell premium — let IV crush work FOR you, not against you.
